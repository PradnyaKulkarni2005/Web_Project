Andhra Pradesh


"A Tapestry of Heritage, and Natural Beauty"

Discover the vibrant tapestry of Andhra Pradesh, where history, culture, and nature converge to create an unforgettable travel experience. From the majestic ruins of Hampi and the serene beauty of Araku Valley to the spiritual aura of Tirupati and the coastal charm of Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh offers a rich blend of heritage, adventure, and relaxation. Whether you seek the thrill of exploring ancient temples or the tranquility of pristine beaches, Andhra Pradesh promises a journey of wonder and delight at every turn.

Historical and Cultural Sites

Tirumala Venkateswara Temple, Tirupati

A major pilgrimage site and one of the richest temples in the world.

Charminar, Hyderabad

A historic monument and mosque known for its architectural beauty.

Lepakshi

Renowned for its Veerabhadra Temple, featuring exquisite sculptures and mural paintings.

Amaravati

Famous for the ancient Buddhist stupa and the new capital city of Andhra Pradesh.

Beaches and Coastal Areas

Visakhapatnam

Popular beaches like Ramakrishna Beach, Rushikonda Beach, and the scenic Yarada Beach.

Bheemunipatnam

A serene beach town with historical significance.

Kakinada

Known for its beautiful beaches and the nearby Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary.

Hill Stations

Araku Valley

A picturesque hill station with coffee plantations, waterfalls, and tribal culture.

Horsley Hills

Known for its pleasant climate, scenic beauty, and adventure activities.

Natural Attractions

Borra Caves

Limestone caves with impressive stalactites and stalagmites near Araku Valley.

Belum Caves

The longest cave system in India open to the public, known for its unique formations.

Ethipothala Falls

A spectacular waterfall near Nagarjuna Sagar Dam.

Religious Sites

Srisailam

A major pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the Nallamala Hills.

Kanaka Durga Temple, Vijayawada

Situated on Indrakeeladri Hill, overlooking the Krishna River.

Simhachalam Temple, Visakhapatnam

An important temple dedicated to Lord Varaha Narasimha.

Wildlife and Nature Reserves

Kambalakonda Wildlife Sanctuary

Home to diverse flora and fauna.

Papikondalu National Park

Scenic boat rides along the Godavari River with lush green hills.

Pulicat Lake

A large saltwater lagoon on the border of Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu, ideal for bird watching.

Heritage Sites

Chandragiri Fort

A historical fort near Tirupati, known for its impressive architecture and scenic views.

Gandikota Fort

Often referred to as the Grand Canyon of India, known for its stunning gorge views.

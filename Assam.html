ASSAM


"Where Natural Beauty Meets Cultural Splendor"

Welcome to Assam, where the mystical landscapes of the Northeast unfold in all their splendor. Nestled in the embrace of the mighty Brahmaputra River, Assam is a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers alike. Explore lush tea gardens that carpet the rolling hills, where the aroma of freshly brewed Assam tea fills the air. Immerse yourself in the vibrant culture of diverse indigenous tribes, their traditional music, and exquisite handicrafts. Witness the breathtaking Kaziranga National Park, home to the one-horned rhinoceros and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Whether you seek tranquility or adventure, Assam promises an unforgettable journey through its untouched beauty and warm hospitality.

Natural Attractions

Kaziranga National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its population of the endangered one-horned rhinoceros.

Manas National Park

Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its biodiversity and scenic beauty.

Majuli Island

One of the world's largest river islands, located on the Brahmaputra River.

Cultural and Historical Sites

Sivasagar

An ancient city with a rich history, once the capital of the Ahom Kingdom.

Kamakhya Temple

A renowned Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Kamakhya, located on the Nilachal Hill in Guwahati.

Hajo

A town known for its religious harmony, housing temples and mosques.

Scenic and Adventure Spots

Haflong

The only hill station in Assam, known for its picturesque landscapes.

Dibru-Saikhowa National Park

Known for its unique ecosystem and as a biodiversity hotspot.

Tea Gardens of Jorhat

Jorhat is known as the tea capital of Assam, with vast tea gardens.

Ranakpur Jain Temple, Pali

Renowned for its intricate marble architecture and sculptures.

Urban and Leisure Spots

Guwahati

The largest city in Assam, offering a mix of urban attractions and natural beauty.

Silchar

Known for its cultural diversity and educational institutions.

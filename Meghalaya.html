MEGHALAYA


"Nature's Paradise and Cultural Haven"

Nestled in the lush hills of Northeast India, Meghalaya beckons with its serene beauty and rich cultural tapestry. Known as the "Abode of Clouds," this enchanting state boasts cascading waterfalls that plunge into emerald-green pools, ancient living root bridges that defy time, and mist-covered valleys that whisper tales of a bygone era. Explore the vibrant markets of Shillong, the capital city resonating with music and cosmopolitan charm, or trek through the Khasi and Jaintia Hills where each step reveals breathtaking vistas. Whether you seek adventure or tranquility, Meghalaya promises an unforgettable journey into nature's embrace, where every moment unfolds like a chapter from a fairy tale.

Natural Wonders

Living Root Bridges

Located in Cherrapunji and Mawlynnong, these unique bridges are made from the roots of rubber trees.

Nohkalikai Falls

Near Cherrapunji, it is the tallest plunge waterfall in India.

Umiam Lake

A beautiful man-made reservoir near Shillong, popular for water sports and scenic views.

Elephant Falls

Near Shillong, known for its multi-tiered falls and scenic beauty.

Caves

Mawsmai Cave

Near Cherrapunji, known for its limestone formations and accessible passages.

Liat Prah Cave

Part of the Siju Cave system, it is the longest natural cave in India.

Siju Cave

Located near Naphak Lake, known for its bat population and limestone formations.

Cultural and Historical Sites

Shillong Peak

Offers panoramic views of Shillong city and surrounding areas.

Don Bosco Museum

In Shillong, showcases the culture and heritage of Northeast India.

Nartiang Monoliths

Located in Jaintia Hills, these are a collection of large monoliths with historical significance.

Hill Stations

Shillong

Known as the "Scotland of the East," famous for its cool climate, colonial architecture, and vibrant culture.

Cherrapunji

Famous for its heavy rainfall, living root bridges, and beautiful landscapes.

Eco-Tourism and Villages

Mawlynnong Village

Known as the cleanest village in Asia, famous for its cleanliness and community-based eco-tourism.

Dawki

Famous for the Umngot River with crystal clear waters, it is a popular spot for boating and experiencing the Indo-Bangladesh border.

Wildlife and Nature Reserves

Balpakram National Park

Known for its unique landscape and rich biodiversity.

Nokrek National Park

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, home to various species of flora and fauna including the red panda.

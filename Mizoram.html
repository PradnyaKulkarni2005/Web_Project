MIZORAM


Explore Nature's Bounty and Rich Culture in Northeast India

Welcome to Mizoram, where lush green hills meet serene landscapes and vibrant cultures. Nestled in the northeastern part of India, Mizoram offers a unique blend of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. From the picturesque hill stations like Aizawl and Champhai to the tranquil lakes such as Palak Dil and Tamdil, every corner of Mizoram invites you to explore its pristine beauty. Immerse yourself in the warm hospitality of the Mizos, indulge in traditional delicacies, and witness colorful festivals that reflect the state's deep-rooted traditions. Whether you seek adventure in trekking through dense forests or peace in the midst of breathtaking vistas, Mizoram promises an unforgettable journey into the heart of Northeast India.

Natural Attractions

Phawngpui National Park

Known for its beautiful landscape and rich biodiversity.

Vantawng Falls

The highest waterfall in Mizoram, surrounded by lush greenery.

Tamdil Lake

A serene lake ideal for boating and picnics.

Dampa Tiger Reserve

A wildlife sanctuary home to diverse flora and fauna.

Cultural and Historical Sites

Reiek Tlang

A mountain with a traditional Mizo village and viewpoints offering stunning vistas.

Luangmual Handicrafts Centre

Showcases traditional Mizo crafts and culture.

Solomon’s Temple

An impressive religious site and architectural marvel.

Urban and Entertainment

Aizawl

The capital city, offering a mix of urban attractions, local markets, and cultural spots.

Zarkawt

A bustling area in Aizawl known for its shops, restaurants, and vibrant atmosphere.

Religious Sites

Khawnglung Wildlife Sanctuary

Besides its natural beauty, it is a place of religious significance for the locals.

Bung Bung Hills

Important for both its natural beauty and its spiritual significance to the Mizo people.

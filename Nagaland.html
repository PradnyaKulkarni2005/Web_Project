NAGALAND


Where Culture Meets Adventure in India's Northeast

Nestled in the lush hills of Northeast India, Nagaland beckons with its rich tapestry of culture, adventure, and natural beauty. This hidden gem boasts vibrant festivals like the Hornbill Festival, where ancient traditions come alive amidst colorful dances and folk songs. From the serene Dzukou Valley to the rugged peaks of Mount Saramati, adventure enthusiasts find endless trails to explore. Immerse yourself in the warmth of Naga hospitality and savor local delicacies while discovering the unique heritage of Nagaland. Experience a journey like no other in this enchanting land where every moment unveils a new chapter of discovery and wonder.

Natural Attractions

Dzukou Valley

Known for its breathtaking landscapes and seasonal flowers, this valley is a popular trekking destination.

Mount Saramati

The highest peak in Nagaland, offering challenging treks and panoramic views.

Shilloi Lake

A serene and picturesque lake located in Phek district, surrounded by lush greenery.

Cultural and Historical Sites

Kisama Heritage Village

Home to the famous Hornbill Festival, showcasing Naga culture, traditions, and handicrafts.

War Cemetery in Kohima

A memorial to the soldiers who died in World War II during the Battle of Kohima.

Khonoma Village

Known as Asia's first green village, it is a model for sustainable and eco-friendly living.

Wildlife and Conservation Areas

Intanki National Park

A protected area with rich biodiversity, including rare and endangered species of flora and fauna.

Pulie Badze Wildlife Sanctuary

Offers opportunities for bird watching and hiking amidst beautiful natural surroundings.

Urban Attractions

Kohima

The state capital, featuring attractions- Kohima War Cemetery, the State Museum,local markets.

Dimapur

The largest city in Nagaland, known for its ancient Kachari Ruins, bustling markets, and Dimapur Ao Baptist Church.

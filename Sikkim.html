SIKKIM


A Paradise for Nature Lovers and Adventure Seekers

Nestled in the heart of the Eastern Himalayas, Sikkim is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its breathtaking landscapes, pristine lakes, and vibrant culture, this enchanting state offers an unparalleled experience for every traveler. From the towering peaks of Kanchenjunga to the serene monasteries and lush tea gardens, Sikkim promises a journey of tranquility and adventure. Whether you're seeking a spiritual retreat, thrilling treks, or simply a peaceful escape into nature, Sikkim's unique charm will captivate your soul and leave you longing for more.

Natural Attractions

Tsomgo Lake

A stunning glacial lake situated at an altitude of 3,753 meters.

Gurudongmar Lake

One of the highest lakes in the world, located at an altitude of 5,183 meters.

Yumthang Valley

Known as the "Valley of Flowers," it is home to a variety of flora and fauna.

Nathula Pass

A mountain pass on the Indo-China border offering breathtaking views.

Monasteries and Spiritual Sites

Rumtek Monastery

One of the most important seats of the Kagyu sect of Buddhism.

Pemayangtse Monastery

One of the oldest and premier monasteries in Sikkim.

Tashiding Monastery

A significant Buddhist monastery known for its annual Bumchu Festival.

Adventure and Trekking

Goechala Trek

A popular trekking route offering stunning views of Mount Kanchenjunga.

Dzongri Trek

A shorter but equally scenic trek leading to Dzongri La Pass.

Sandakphu Trek

Offers panoramic views of the world's highest peaks, including Everest and Kanchenjunga.

Towns and Villages

Gangtok

The capital city, known for its vibrant culture, shopping, and views of the Himalayas.

Pelling

Offers magnificent views of Mount Kanchenjunga and is home to several monasteries.

Lachung

A picturesque mountain village known for its apple orchards and scenic beauty.

Lakes and Waterfalls

Khecheopalri Lake

A sacred lake believed to fulfill wishes.

Seven Sisters Waterfalls

A series of seven waterfalls, offering a spectacular sight especially during the monsoon.

Banjhakri Falls

A popular tourist spot with a park showcasing Sikkimese culture and traditions.

Cultural and Historical Sites

Namgyal Institute of Tibetology

A research institute and museum dedicated to Tibetan culture.

Rabdentse Ruins

The remnants of the former capital of the Kingdom of Sikkim.

Sikkim Himalayan Zoological Park

Home to various Himalayan species, including the red panda.

Hot Springs and Relaxation

Yumthang Hot Springs

Natural hot springs located in the Yumthang Valley.

Phurchachu Reshi Hot Spring

Known for its therapeutic properties, located near a sacred cave.

Wildlife and Nature Parks

Khangchendzonga National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its biodiversity and stunning landscapes.

Shingba Rhododendron Sanctuary

Home to numerous species of rhododendrons and other flora.

TRIPURA


"Unveiling the Hidden Gem of Northeast India"

Tripura beckons travelers with its rich cultural tapestry and natural splendor. From the majestic Ujjayanta Palace in Agartala, resonating with royal history, to the tranquil lakes and lush green valleys of Jampui Hills, every corner of Tripura offers a unique charm. Delve into the vibrant tribal culture, savor local delicacies like Mui Borok, and explore ancient temples like the Mata Tripurasundari Temple in Udaipur. Whether you seek adventure in wildlife sanctuaries or seek solace in its peaceful ambiance, Tripura promises an unforgettable journey into India's hidden gem.

Historical and Cultural Sites

Ujjayanta Palace

A former royal palace located in Agartala, showcasing the grandeur of Tripura's royal heritage.

Neermahal Palace

A magnificent water palace situated in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake, blending Hindu and Mughal architectural styles.

Tripura Sundari Temple

One of the 51 Shakti Peethas, this temple is an important pilgrimage site dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari.

Unakoti

Known for its ancient rock carvings and sculptures, this site is a significant archaeological and pilgrimage destination.

Natural Attractions

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

A biodiversity hotspot that offers a variety of flora and fauna, including the unique spectacled monkey.

Dumboor Lake

A picturesque lake ideal for boating and bird watching, surrounded by lush greenery and small islets.

Jampui Hills

Known as the "Land of Eternal Spring," these hills offer panoramic views, lush landscapes, and orange orchards.

Adventure and Eco-Tourism

Baramura Eco-Park

A serene eco-park that offers trekking and nature walks amidst rich biodiversity.

Chabimura

Famous for its rock carvings on the steep mountain walls along the banks of the Gomati River, accessible via a boat ride.

Cultural Experience

Tribal Culture Museum

Located in Agartala, this museum provides insight into the rich tribal culture and heritage of Tripura.

Heritage Park

A thematic park in Agartala showcasing miniaturized replicas of Tripura's iconic monuments and cultural landmarks.

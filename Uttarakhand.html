UTTARAKHAND


"Where Adventure Meets Serenity"

Nestled in the lap of the majestic Himalayas, Uttarakhand is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. This enchanting state, often called "Devbhoomi" or the "Land of Gods," boasts pristine landscapes, lush green valleys, and serene lakes. From the spiritual tranquility of Rishikesh and Haridwar to the snowy escapades in Auli and the tiger trails in Jim Corbett National Park, Uttarakhand promises an unforgettable journey that blends spirituality, adventure, and breathtaking natural beauty. Discover the magic of Uttarakhand, where every moment is a step closer to heaven.

Hill Stations

Mussoorie

Known as the "Queen of the Hills," it's famous for its scenic beauty, Kempty Falls, and Camel's Back Road.

Nainital

Renowned for its beautiful lakes, especially Naini Lake, and viewpoints like Snow View Point.

Ranikhet

A peaceful hill station with panoramic views of the Himalayas, Chaubatia Gardens, and Jhula Devi Temple.

Almora

Offers a blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty, with places like Zero Point, Jageshwar Dham, and Katarmal Sun Temple.

Kausani

Known for its stunning sunrise and sunset views, it’s often called the 'Switzerland of India'.

Pilgrimage Sites

Haridwar

A major pilgrimage site with the famous Har Ki Pauri ghat and the evening Ganga Aarti.

Rishikesh

Known as the 'Yoga Capital of the World', it’s also famous for the Laxman Jhula, and Ram Jhula.

Badrinath

One of the Char Dham temples, dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Kedarnath

Another Char Dham site, known for the Kedarnath Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Gangotri

Origin of the River Ganga and one of the Char Dham

Yamunotri

Source of the Yamuna River and also part of the Char Dham.

Wildlife and Nature

Jim Corbett National Park

India's oldest national park, known for its tiger population and diverse wildlife.

Valley of Flowers

A UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its meadows of endemic alpine flowers and a variety of flora.

Rajaji National Park

Rich in wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and leopards.

Binsar Sanctuary

Offers stunning views of the Himalayan peaks and rich biodiversity.

Adventure and Sports

Auli

Famous for skiing, it offers some of the best slopes in Asia.

Rishikesh

Besides being a spiritual hub, it's also known for adventure activities like white-water rafting, bungee jumping, and trekking.

Chopta

Known as the 'Mini Switzerland of India', it's a base for trekking to Tungnath and Chandrashila.

Mukteshwar

Known for rock climbing, rappelling, and camping.

Cultural and Historical

Dehradun

The state capital with attractions like Forest Research Institute, Robber’s Cave, and Mindrolling Monastery.

Almora

Besides natural beauty, it is known for its cultural heritage and temples.

Pithoragarh

Known for its forts, temples, and scenic beauty.

Lansdowne

A cantonment town with British-era churches and quiet environs.

Lakes and Waterfalls

Nainital Lake

The star attraction of Nainital.

Bhulla Tal

A serene man-made lake in Lansdowne.

Sattal

A group of seven interconnected freshwater lakes.

Kempty Falls

A popular waterfall near Mussoorie.

Corbett Waterfalls

A picturesque waterfall in the Jim Corbett National Park area.

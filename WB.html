West Bengal


A Tapestry of Culture, Heritage, and Natural Splendor

Discover the enchanting allure of West Bengal, a vibrant tapestry of rich culture, stunning landscapes, and historical grandeur. From the bustling streets of Kolkata, the City of Joy, to the serene tea gardens of Darjeeling, and the mystical Sundarbans, home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger, West Bengal offers an unforgettable journey through diverse experiences. Immerse yourself in the artistic heritage, savor the delectable cuisine, and embrace the warm hospitality that makes this state a true gem for every traveler. Explore West Bengal, where every corner tells a story waiting to be uncovered.

Cultural and Historical Sites

Victoria Memorial, Kolkata

Iconic museum and memorial dedicated to Queen Victoria.

Indian Museum, Kolkata

Oldest and largest museum in India with extensive exhibits.

Belur Math, Howrah

Headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission, showcasing architectural beauty and spiritual heritage.

Dakshineswar Kali Temple, Kolkata

Famous temple dedicated to Goddess Kali on the banks of River Hooghly.

Cooch Behar Palace

Magnificent royal palace known for its architecture and historical significance.

Natural and Scenic Beauty

Sundarbans National Park

World's largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for Bengal tigers.

Darjeeling

Hill station famous for tea gardens, panoramic views of the Himalayas, and the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (UNESCO World Heritage).

Jaldapara National Park

Wildlife sanctuary known for one-horned rhinos, elephants, and varied birdlife.

Mirik

Hill station known for its serene lake surrounded by pine trees and tea estates.

Religious Sites

Kalighat Kali Temple, Kolkata

Ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Kali.

Tarakeshwar Temple, Hooghly

Famous temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its annual fair.

Bishnupur Temples, Bankura

Terracotta temples dedicated to Lord Vishnu, showcasing exquisite craftsmanship.

ISKCON Mayapur, Nadia

Headquarters of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, a major pilgrimage site.

Hill Stations

Darjeeling

Known for its tea gardens, toy train, and views of Kanchenjunga.

Kalimpong

Quieter hill station known for its orchids, Buddhist monasteries, and panoramic views.

Sandakphu

Highest peak in West Bengal, popular for trekking and stunning views of Mount Everest and Kanchenjunga.

Lava and Loleygaon

Lesser-known hill stations offering tranquility and views of the Himalayas.

Beaches

Digha

One of the most popular seaside resorts in West Bengal.

Mandarmani

Known for its long beach and tranquil surroundings.

Tajpur

Lesser-known beach destination offering peace and natural beauty.

Bakkhali

Secluded beach destination near the Sundarbans with a unique charm.
